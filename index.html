About Ψ-Theory

About Me

I am an independent researcher from Kaliningrad working on late–time cosmology and the physics of non-equilibrium systems. My main focus is the development of the Psi-Continuum framework — a macroscopic response–based approach that explores whether certain gravitational and cosmological phenomena can emerge from deeper phase-like properties of the cosmic medium. My work aims to remain strictly within the standards of reproducible scientific methodology: all numerical tests, cosmological likelihoods, and datasets associated with the computational releases are fully open-source.

My primary work is the development of the Psi-Continuum Theory — a macroscopic framework describing gravity, energy flow, and phase-like structure across physical scales.

Research Topics

  • phenomenology of late-time cosmology
  • background expansion tests and model comparison
  • non-equilibrium response frameworks
  • gradient-driven structure and energy transport
  • future extensions toward perturbations and mesoscopic dynamics

Psi-Continuum v2 Package

Final archived research release · reproducible computational framework

The Psi-Continuum v2 package is an open-source computational framework for testing the Ψ-CDM model against modern cosmological datasets. It includes:

  • ΛCDM and ΨCDM background solvers
  • full likelihood pipeline: SN Ia, H(z), BAO (SDSS + DESI)
  • joint χ² comparison tools
  • ε₀ parameter scanning
  • publication-ready figure generator
  • interactive CLI for new users
